ABOUT US
Turning Struggles Into Strengths, Together
Our journey
You wouldn't go to a car mechanic for chest pains.

So why should someone fighting addiction, homelessness, or reentry take their guidance from people who have only read about it? My Struggle was founded in 2021 by Brian Reinhart and Wayne Giles on one conviction: lived experience is expertise.

Every mentor in our program has been through the struggle themselves. Every program we run — outreach centers, Position of Neutrality, QR Code Giving, transitional housing — was built by people who needed it once and didn't have it.

We started in Laveen, Arizona, with a folding table and a question: "How can I help you?" That question is still the whole model.

Our mission
what is My Struggle

My Struggle is a nonprofit founded in 2021 in Laveen, Arizona, by people who have lived through homelessness, addiction, and incarceration — and found their way home.

We believe nobody escapes the struggle alone. Our outreach centers pair every member with a peer mentor who has walked the same road, because the person best equipped to guide you out is someone who has been where you are.

Through QR Code Giving, anyone can give directly to a member's journey — half in cash they redeem at our centers, half in credits for essentials at The Store. Every gift is accountable, dignified, and personal.

Our register is simple: "How can I help you? Let's talk — tell me about you." Compassion replacing contempt, one member at a time.

Founded in 2021 in Laveen, Arizona, by people who lived through homelessness, addiction, and incarceration — and found their way home. Nobody escapes the struggle alone: every member gets a peer mentor who has walked the same road.
